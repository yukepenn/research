## Where our distinct contribution survives

Across the verified neighborhood, **no single paper combines our four deltas, and most miss three of them.** The literature splits cleanly into camps that each touch one axis: (a) cross-model/multi-agent code critique exists and is useful (cc01 anchor, cc09, cc10, cc11, cc12, and the unverified cc02 twin) â€” but none runs a *strict equal-budget* comparison against simply giving the author more compute, and none builds a *full bidirectional* authorâ†”reviewer matrix to separate "stronger reviewer" from "complementary reviewer"; (b) budget-matched skepticism about multi-agent gains exists (cc05) and even a closed-form theory tying *budget + error correlation* to synergy exists (cc04) â€” but in reasoning/synthetic settings, with scalar correlation, not *per-defect-type* authorâ†”reviewer correlation on repository patches; (c) error correlation among LLMs is empirically established (cc06) and self-review is shown unreliable (cc07, cc08) â€” establishing our *premise* but not our *mechanism*; and (d) model routing for code exists (cc03) and disagreement-as-signal exists (cc13) â€” but they route *which model generates*, not the *pre-call decision over review actions* (`no-review/self/same-family/cross-family/test-gen/reimplementation`) evaluated on held-out repositories with a budget-matched correctness endpoint.

Our surviving contribution is the **conjunction**: a budget-matched, fully bidirectional authorâ€“reviewer matrix over multiple model families on repository-level defective patches, where we (1) model error correlation *at the defect-type level* to explain *when* heterogeneity is complementary rather than merely "more compute," and (2) turn that complementarity into a *pre-call router* validated on held-out repositories with a final-patch-correctness (not detection-only) endpoint, including a regression-rate guard. cc04 (theory) and cc03 (code routing) are the deltas most likely to be cited as "already done," so the paper must foreground the code-specific, defect-type-conditioned, authorâ†”reviewer empirical instantiation and the budget-matched correctness endpoint that neither provides.
