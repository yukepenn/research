# DeltaResearch — Novelty Matrix (nearest-neighbor sweep)

Paper: **p3_deltaresearch — DeltaResearch: Selective Revision of Deep-Research Reports under Evidence Updates**

Central claim (restated): when underlying *evidence* is locally updated / corrected / retracted / made conflicting, deep-research agents either miss stale conclusions OR damage large amounts of correct content; representing the report as a **typed claim-evidence dependency graph** and generating a **constrained minimal patch** improves BOTH "change what must change" (affected-claim recall, incl. multi-hop derived/temporal/computed claims) and "preserve what must not" (unaffected-claim preservation), while maintaining citation + calculation validity and honestly representing conflict.

Sweep date: 2026-06-25. Every paper below was fetched and verified (`verified=true` in `related_work.csv`). The clinical "living systematic review" automation literature (researchsquare / PMC) was scanned but is workflow/medical, not a benchmark or method matching our claim object, so it is not counted as a neighbor.

## Per-dimension reading vs OUR claim

- **Problem.** Only `editpropbench_2026` and `fruit_2022` share the exact "a local change implies obligated text revisions while protecting the rest" framing. `mrdre_2026` shares the change-vs-preserve *phenomenon* on DR reports but with the wrong trigger (user feedback). The temporal/conflict/model-editing cluster (`evolveqa`, `ramdocs`, `rippleedits`, `chainedit`) shares sub-problems (temporal staleness, conflict, derived-fact ripple) but on QA / parameters, never on a long-form report revised against an *evidence delta*.
- **Data.** No existing benchmark pairs an **evidence world W_t -> W_{t+1} delta** with **gold A/U/N/C claim sets over a deep-research report**, mixing controlled worlds AND real versioned public sources. `editpropbench` (synthetic manuscripts) and `evolveqa`/`deepfact` (real timestamped/ versioned) each hold one half; none combines both for report revision.
- **Method.** A **typed claim-evidence dependency graph** (supports / derives / qualifies / contradicts / temporal-validity edges) feeding a **constrained minimal patch generator with audit trail** is unclaimed. `chainedit`/`rippleedits` use graphs/rules but for parameters; `aar_auditable_2026` uses a provenance graph but for generation-time auditability, not patching under a delta.
- **Metric.** A single endpoint that **jointly penalizes stale residual (missed A) and spurious revision (damaged U)**, plus derived/temporal/computed-claim correctness and citation+calculation validity, is distinctive. `editpropbench` (ERA + protected-text) is the only one with a comparable dual-sided metric, but it lacks temporal-validity, citation-validity, computed-claim, and honest-conflict (C) components.
- **Model/object.** Our object is a **deep-research agent revising a report under an external evidence update**. `mrdre`/`deepfact`/`aar` share the DR-agent object but not the evidence-delta-driven selective revision; `editpropbench`/`fruit` share document revision but not the agent/evidence-world setting.
- **Contribution.** The combination — evidence-world delta -> gold affected/unaffected/new/conflicting claim sets, multi-hop derived + temporal + computed claims, typed-graph-guided minimal patch + audit trail, and a metric that punishes both omission and collateral damage — is not assembled by any single neighbor.

## Where our distinct contribution survives

The dangerous neighbor is **EditPropBench (2605.02083): high collision.** It independently formalizes "a local factual edit creates non-local revision obligations," ships a controlled fact graph with gold *direct-target / required-downstream / protected-unrelated* labels, scores both required updates (ERA) and protected text, and reports the same headline ("strongest editors still miss ~30% of cascade updates"). If a reviewer wanted to say "already done," this is the paper. We must cite it as the closest prior work and frame DeltaResearch precisely against it. Our contribution still survives on five axes EditPropBench does not touch: (1) the input change lives in an **external evidence world** (numeric revision, date/validity change, **source retraction**, **new more-authoritative source**, **two-source conflict**, definition change, upstream-fact-triggered recomputation, evidence deletion-but-still-supported) rather than an author-supplied manuscript edit; (2) **honest representation of conflict/uncertainty (the C set)** and **temporal-validity** reasoning, absent from EditPropBench; (3) **citation and calculation validity maintenance** across the update; (4) **real versioned public evidence (Layer B)** alongside controlled worlds, vs EditPropBench's synthetic-only manuscripts; and (5) a **typed-edge dependency-graph METHOD with claim-level patch + audit trail driving a deep-research agent**, vs EditPropBench's benchmark-with-protocols (no typed-graph method, no agent, no audit).

Against **Mr.DRE (high, phenomenon-level)** the carve-out is already by design: evidence-world delta vs user feedback, plus our gold A/U/N/C sets, dependency graph, patch, and joint stale+spurious metric. Against the remaining medium/low neighbors the surviving delta is consistent: **FRUIT** has the update-given-evidence task but no dependency graph / preserve-penalty / derived-temporal claims; **DeepFact** and **AAR** give versioned/auditable claim-evidence machinery for DR but only verify/provenance at generation time rather than revise under a delta; **RippleEdits / ChainEdit** own the derived-fact ripple but in parameter space via QA; **evolveQA / RAMDocs** own temporal evolution and conflict but as QA, not report revision. Net: the *assembly* — evidence-delta -> gold affected/unaffected/new/conflicting report-claim sets, multi-hop derived/temporal/computed claims, typed-graph-guided minimal patch + audit, and a metric penalizing both stale residual and collateral damage — is currently unclaimed. **Recommended action:** treat EditPropBench as the primary differentiation target (add a direct head-to-head positioning paragraph and, if feasible, a comparison or adaptation of ERA-style metrics to the evidence-world setting), and keep the evidence-world/retraction/conflict/temporal/citation/agent framing front-and-center to avoid a "manuscript edit propagation, but for reports" reduction.
