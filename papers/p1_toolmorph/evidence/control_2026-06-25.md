# P1 ToolMorph — information-matched control (2026-06-25)

The decisive go/no-go: is the enum-encoding degradation due to the CHANGED REPRESENTATION (which a
label-free STNF could normalize) or due to HIDDEN INFORMATION (which it cannot)? We compare the
original interface, enum-opaque (string enums replaced by opaque codes c0,c1,...), and enum-legend
(the SAME opaque codes but with a legend in the tool description restoring c0=low, etc. — identical
representation, information restored). Real Claude+Codex, interactive harness, 2 reps, 10 tasks.
Ledger: `artifacts/p1_control_ledger.jsonl`.

## Result (paired vs original, task-clustered CI, n=20)
| condition | Claude | Δ (CI) | Codex | Δ (CI) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| original | 1.00 | — | 1.00 | — |
| enum-opaque | 0.80 | −0.20 [−0.50, 0.00] | 1.00 | 0.00 |
| **enum-legend (info restored, same codes)** | **1.00** | **0.00** | **1.00** | **0.00** |

## Reading (decisive)
- Restoring the information (a legend) while keeping the coded representation **eliminates the
  degradation entirely** (Claude enum 0.80 → 1.00). The effect is **information-hiding, not
  representation.**
- Combined with the earlier finding that the other 5 strict transforms (nesting, lexical, etc.)
  cause NO drop, the honest conclusion is: **on these tasks, frontier agents (Claude, Codex) are
  robust to behaviour-preserving interface REPRESENTATION changes; the only apparent fragility comes
  from hidden information, consistent with Schema First (arXiv:2603.13404, semantics not
  representation is the bottleneck).**
- **This triggers P1's pre-registered KILL gate for the STNF method:** content-matching (the legend)
  erases the effect, AND a label-free STNF cannot recover information that has been hidden (opaque
  codes have no recoverable meaning without a legend or executable probes). So the STNF "recovers
  representation-induced degradation" thesis (H5, the surviving novel core) is NOT supported — there
  is no representation-only degradation to recover.

## What survives (the honest contribution)
- **P1-C0 (SUPPORTED):** a property-verified state-transition-equivalence benchmark (6 transform
  families × 3 environments, >15,000 cases, zero mismatch, metamorphic negative control). Real, delivered.
- **A clean negative result:** a controlled adjudication that representation-equivalent,
  information-preserving interface changes do not degrade frontier tool-using agents on these tasks;
  the one apparent effect is attributable to information content. This is a useful, honest finding.
- The STNF method is NOT demonstrated and is retired per the gate.

## Implication: P1 terminal = ARXIV_ONLY (benchmark + negative result), method retired.
Scope/limits: 10 easy tasks at a 1.00 ceiling, 2 model families, single environment family, the enum
effect itself non-significant. A harder-task version could revisit whether representation EVER matters,
but the current evidence says: for these models/tasks, it does not, and STNF has nothing to recover.
