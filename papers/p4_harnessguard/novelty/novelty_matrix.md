# HarnessGuard (p4) -- Novelty Matrix

**Central claim under test.** After an agent-harness component (prompt / tools / middleware / memory / retry / verifier / stopping) is automatically edited, local gains often hide cross-task regressions. *Structurally representing the edit*, *selecting a few high-risk diverse canaries from historical task traces*, and *escalating to full eval when uncertain* finds most dangerous regressions at far below full-benchmark cost.

**Our four-part contribution (the novelty boundary).** Given a candidate edit: (1) predict which tasks regress BEFORE the expensive full suite; (2) maximize regression detection at a fixed canary budget k<<|D|; (3) abstain/escalate when unsafe; (4) validate on held-out edit type / task family / model family. We do NOT claim: automatic harness editing (AHE, Self-Harness, VeRO), edit self-prediction (AHE decision observability), generic self-evolving agents, smoke/random canaries, or "harness affects ranking".

Verified anchor: Agentic Harness Engineering (AHE), https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25850.

## Where our distinct contribution survives

The literature splits into four camps, none of which solves our problem and which our central claim sits *between*. (1) **Automatic harness optimizers** -- AHE (nn01), Self-Harness (nn02), VeRO (nn05) -- produce the edits we take as input; AHE even records an edit self-prediction. The closest, Self-Harness, already gates edits with regression tests and reports that an agent's *own* regression prediction is barely above random and that it can justify why an edit should help but cannot name what it will break. That is our motivation, not our method: none of them poses, much less optimizes, the question "which few high-risk diverse tasks should I run, conditioned on the structural edit, to catch most regressions at a fixed budget, and when should I abstain and escalate?" (2) **Phenomenon characterizers** -- When-Prompts-Hurt (nn04), Beyond-the-Mean (nn12), Harness-Updating (nn03) -- confirm that edits/upgrades cause large, partly noise-masked per-task churn, strengthening our RQ1/H1 and our stochastic regression-label definition, but they only measure after running the full suite. (3) **Cheap-evaluation / outcome-prediction** -- Active-Evaluation-Acquisition (nn07) and 100-Instances (nn06) -- are the strongest *method* neighbors and our must-beat baselines: they select a few items and infer the rest. But they estimate *one model's aggregate or absolute performance*, not the *pass->fail regression delta between two harness versions*; their selection is not conditioned on a structured edit; and 100-Instances even concludes random selection is adequate, which directly contradicts our H2 that regressions are structured and concentrated, so selection matters. (4) **Decision/regression machinery** -- conformal risk control / SCoRE (nn09), sequential canary testing (nn08), positive-congruent training (nn10), and the SE ML-TSP survey (nn11) -- give us reusable abstention, sequential-escalation, regression definitions, and the strongest reproducible test-selection baseline, but none targets stochastic LLM-agent harness edits with model x harness interactions, full-suite regression ground truth, and held-out generalization across edit type / task family / model family.

The surviving, defensible delta is the **composition**: an *edit-conditioned, budget-constrained, diverse canary selector that targets the regression (delta) label rather than absolute performance, wrapped in calibrated abstention/escalation, and validated out-of-distribution across edit/task/model families* -- exactly the seam left open between harness optimizers (which create edits and self-predict poorly), phenomenon papers (which only measure post-hoc), and generic cheap-eval/test-selection methods (which estimate aggregate performance of a single system and find selection unimportant).

### Highest collision risks to manage
- **nn02 Self-Harness (high):** pre-empt with "regression testing as an accept gate" vs "edit-conditioned canary *selection* + calibrated abstention + held-out generalization"; reuse its near-random self-prediction result as motivation, and beat its regression-test gate on recall-at-fixed-budget.
- **nn07 Active-Evaluation-Acquisition + nn06 100-Instances (medium, method twins):** must be implemented as baselines; differentiate on regression-delta target, edit conditioning, and the selection-matters result.
- **nn11 ML-TSP survey (medium):** implement the strongest reproducible SE test-selection baseline and frame the stochastic-agent / edit-semantics / model-family gap explicitly.
- **nn04 When-Prompts-Hurt (medium):** cite as evidence the phenomenon is real (do not claim discovering it); our novelty is foresight, not characterization.
